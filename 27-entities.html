27. Entities

Reserved characters in HTML must be replaced with character entities.

Characters that are not present on your keyboard can also be replaced by entities. Some characters are reserved in HTML - if you use < or > in your text your browser will mix them with tags.

Character entities are used to display reserved characters in HTML.:


To display the less than sign, we must write & lt; or & #60; (without spaces).

Non-breaking Space

A common character entity used in HTML is the non-breaking space, or & nbsp; (again without the space) - this is a space that will not break into a new line. This avoids browsers from truncating multiple spaces

This is similar to the non-breaking hyphen (& #8209;) lets you use a hyphen character (-) that won't break
